How to read this dashboard

This dashboard tracks what prediction markets say about the probability of a US military strike on Iran. Prediction markets are platforms where people bet real money on whether future events will happen. Because participants have money on the line, prices tend to reflect genuine beliefs about probabilities — if a market is priced at 30%, that means traders collectively estimate a 30% chance the event will occur.

The data comes from Polymarket, the largest prediction market platform. Polymarket runs individual markets like "Will the US strike Iran by March 31?" — each priced between 0% and 100%. This dashboard combines all those markets into a single probability curve showing how likely a strike is by any given date.

"Strike by date" (the default view) shows the running total: "What is the probability of a strike happening by this date or earlier?" "Strike on date" shows the daily probability: "How likely is a strike on this particular date?"

Important: Prediction markets are not crystal balls. They reflect the crowd's best guess, which can be wrong. Markets can be swayed by low liquidity, misinformation, or sudden news events. Treat these numbers as one signal among many, not as ground truth. For a deeper explanation of how prediction markets work and their limitations, see Scott Alexander's Prediction Market FAQ.
